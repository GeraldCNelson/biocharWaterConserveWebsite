# Biochar Impact: Microbial, Nutrient, and System Insights

## 1. Microbial Biomass (signal strength)

### Total Biomass (key pattern)
| Year | S1 | S2 | S3 | S4 | Interpretation |
|------|----|----|----|----|----------------|
| 2023 spring | 1844 | 1307 | 1500 | 1565 | Biochar already higher in S1 |
| 2024 fall | 3332 | 2176 | 2171 | 2114 | Biochar advantage emerges |
| 2025 fall | 7345 | 6521 | 5983 | 4913 | Strong across both biochar strips |

**Result:** Biochar → higher microbial biomass

### Bacteria (fast cycling)
- S1/S2 ratio declines over time but remains >1
- S3/S4 spikes strongly in 2025  
**Interpretation:** Biochar boosts bacterial activity, especially later

### Fungi (structure + P cycling)
- S1/S2 consistently >1  
- S3/S4 spikes strongly in 2025  
**Interpretation:** Biochar supports fungal networks (important for P)

## 2. Plant Tissue Nutrients (what plants accessed)

### Phosphorus (% dry basis)
- Tight range (~0.18–0.36)
- Very small differences between strips  
**Interpretation:** No strong biochar signal in plant P

### Potassium (% dry basis)
- Larger variation
- Biochar strips generally higher  
**Interpretation:** Clear biochar signal in plant K

---

## 3. System Connections

### K system
- Soil: strong depletion patterns  
- Plant: clear biochar advantage  
- Microbes: secondary role  
**Mechanism:** Physical (retention, water, structure)

### P system
- Soil: small changes  
- Plant: stable  
- Microbes: large increase  
**Mechanism:** Biological buffering

---

## 4. Core Insight

Biochar creates two distinct system responses:

- **K → Physical system**
  - Reduced loss  
  - Improved uptake under stress  
- **P → Biological system**
  - Stable soil levels  
  - Increased microbial mediation  

---

## 5. Key Conclusion

- Biochar improves K availability through physical retention  
- Biochar stabilizes P cycling through microbial expansion  
- Increased microbial biomass does not increase P levels, but stabilizes supply  

---

## 6. One-line takeaway

**Biochar increases biological capacity, which stabilizes phosphorus availability rather than increasing it**
