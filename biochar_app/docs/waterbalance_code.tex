\section*{1. Soil Water Content (SWC)}
Given volumetric water content (VWC) measurements $\theta_d$ at sensor depths $d_1, d_2, d_3$,
the weighted average soil water content over the column is
\[
\mathrm{SWC} \;=\; \sum_{i=1}^{3} w_i \,\theta_{d_i},
\]
where $w_i$ are the depth‐weights (e.g.\ $w_1 + w_2 + w_3 = 1$).

\section*{2. Water Volume in Soil Column}
For a soil column of horizontal cross‐sectional area $A$ (e.g.\ strip width $\times$ length),
and volumetric water contents at successive depths, the total water volume $V$ is approximated by:
\[
V \;=\; A \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \frac{\theta_{d_i} + \theta_{d_{i+1}}}{2}\,(z_{i+1} - z_i),
\]
where $z_i$ are the depths (e.g.\ top of reliable range for 6\,in sensor to bottom for 18\,in).

\section*{3. Water Balance}
Over an irrigation event:
\[
\text{Runoff Volume}
\;=\;
\text{Applied Irrigation Volume}
\;-\;
\Delta S_{\text{soil}},
\]
with
\[
\Delta S_{\text{soil}}
\;=\;
A \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \frac{\Delta \theta_{d_i} + \Delta \theta_{d_{i+1}}}{2}\,(z_{i+1} - z_i).
\]

\section*{4. Unit Conversions}
Using volumetric water content $\theta$ (unitless fraction),
to convert soil water volume in cubic meters to liters:
\[
V_{\mathrm{L}} \;=\; V_{\mathrm{m^3}} \times 1000.
\]
