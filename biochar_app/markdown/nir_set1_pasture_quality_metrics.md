# Pasture Quality Metrics (NIR Clean Data — Set 1)

## What Set 1 is for
Set 1 is a compact, interpretable snapshot of forage quality. It emphasizes variables that are:
- widely used in forage science,
- comparable across dates and years,
- and directly useful for comparing treatment effects (e.g., biochar vs. control).

**All variables are reported on a dry-matter basis** to remove the confounding effect of changing moisture content.

## Variables included (Set 1)
| Variable | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Crude Protein (Dry Basis, %)** | Core indicator of forage nutritional value and nitrogen availability. Often sensitive to management and growing conditions. |
| **Acid Detergent Fiber — ADF (Dry Basis, %)** | Less-digestible structural fiber. Higher ADF generally means lower digestibility and energy. |
| **Neutral Detergent Fiber — NDF (Dry Basis, %)** | Total cell-wall content. Higher NDF generally reduces intake potential (rumen fill). |
| **Total Digestible Nutrients — TDN (Dry Basis, %)** | Common energy metric used in ration formulation; integrates multiple forage components. |
| **Relative Feed Value (RFV, unitless)** | Standard index derived from ADF and NDF that summarizes forage quality into one number. |

## Why strip ratios are shown (S1/S2 and S3/S4)
Ratios provide a direct within-block comparison and help reduce the influence of:
- year-to-year climate variability,
- sampling timing effects,
- and absolute calibration offsets.

They are especially useful for detecting **relative differences** consistent with treatment effects.
