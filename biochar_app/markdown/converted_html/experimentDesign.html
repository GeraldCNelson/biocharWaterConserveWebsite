The Biochar Experimental Design

The experiment began in Spring 2023 at the Fruita Research Station of Colorado State University.

The image below shows the layout of the experimental field. It was an unused pasture experiment plot. There are four strips, numbered 1 to 4 from left (west) to right (east). Each strip is about 46 ft wide and 280 ft long.


The Experimental Design

In spring 2023, strips 1 and 3 had biochar injected every 6 inches to an approximate depth of 6 inches. Strips 2 and 4 had no biochar amendment.

Irrigation

Water for the field is done using furrow irrigation, with the furrows 30 inches apart. Irrigation water is applied at the north end (top of the image) and runs down the furrows, infiltrating into the soil until it is saturated. Any excess runs into a drainage ditch at the south end of the field.

Irrigation is applied to the east two rows (strips 3 and 4) roughly every two weeks during the growing season. The two west strips are irrigated only once a month.


Biochar source and application

The biochar used in this experiment is made from pistachio shells and was purchased from VGrid Energy Systems, Camarillo, California. More details and chemical analysis results will be available in the Additional Information tab.

Pistachio shell biochar

The process to collect soil moisture, electrical conductivity, and temperature is described in detail in the Technical Details tab.

Soil sampling

Soil samples for each strip have been collected in early spring and late fall and analyzed by Ward Labs in Kearney, Nebraska. Soils were sampled for both mineral and biological activity, once in the spring and once in the fall.

Soil sampling dates

2024 March 22, November 7
2025 April 2


Fertilizer application

The Ward Labs fertilizer recommendations based on the soil samples for each strip were followed.
