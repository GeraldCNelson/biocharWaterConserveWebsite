<h2 id="hypotheses-to-be-tested">Hypotheses to be tested</h2>
<p>As discussed in the Introduction tab, the process of making biochar leaves massive amounts of space available in the biochar pores where water, nutrients, and soil biota can be stored. In a desert environment like that around Fruita, Colorado, water shortages can be a big problem. The project has three related research questions.</p>
<p>The primary research question is how much more water can be stored in a field with biochar than in a field without and for how long.</p>
<p>A second research question is whether biochar contributes to soil health and the quantity and quality of pasture production.</p>
<p>Finally, do the benefits of having biochar in the soil last for multiple years.</p>
<p>The experiment began in Spring 2023 at the Fruita Research Station of Colorado State University. This writeup covers the period from 2023 to the end of mid 2026.</p>
<h2 id="experiment-design">Experiment design</h2>
<p>The image below shows the layout of the experimental field. It had previously been an unused plot planted to mixed pasture grasses. The field was divided into four strips, numbered 1 to 4 from left (west) to right (east). Each strip is about 50 ft (15 meters) wide and 350 ft (107 meters) long.</p>
<figure>
<img src="/static/images/biocharExperimentalDesign.webp" style="width:3.34525in;height:5.25in"/>
<figcaption>Figure 1. Experiment layout</figcaption>
</figure>
<p>In spring 2023, biochar was injected into strips 1 and 3 every 6 inches to an approximate depth of 6 inches. Strips 2 and 4 had no biochar amendment.</p>
<h2 id="irrigation">Irrigation</h2>
<p>Water for the field is done using furrow irrigation, with the furrows 30 inches apart. Irrigation water is applied at the north end (top of the image) and runs down the furrows, infiltrating into the soil until the soil holding capacity is filled. Any excess runs into a drainage ditch at the south end of the field.</p>
<p>Irrigation is applied to the east two rows (strips 3 and 4) roughly every two weeks during the growing season. The two west strips are irrigated only once a month.</p>
<h2 id="biochar-source-and-application">Biochar source and application</h2>
<p>The biochar used in this experiment is made from pistachio shells and was purchased from <a href="https://vgridenergy.com/">VGrid Energy Systems</a>, Camarillo, California. More details and chemical analysis results will be available in the Additional Information tab.</p>

<p>The process to collect soil moisture, electrical conductivity, and temperature is described in detail in the Technical Details tab.</p>
<h2 id="soil-sampling">Soil sampling</h2>
<p>Soil samples for each strip are collected in early spring and late fall and analyzed by <a href="https://www.wardlab.com/">Ward Labs</a> in Kearney, Nebraska. Soils were sampled for both chemical content and biological activity, once in the spring and once in the fall (Soil <a class="tab-link" data-tab="soilbio-tab" href="#">Biological Health</a> and <a class="tab-link" data-tab="soilchem-tab" href="#">Soil Chemistry</a> tabs. As with all the data presented here, these data can be downloaded using the Bulk Downloads tab.</p>
